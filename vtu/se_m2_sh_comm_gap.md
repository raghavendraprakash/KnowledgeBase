# Examples of Stakeholder Communication Gaps

Effective communication is the cornerstone of successful project management. When gaps occur between stakeholders, project timelines and quality often suffer. Below are two distinct examples illustrating how these gaps manifest and their impact.

## Example 1: The "Technical vs. Business" Vocabulary Gap
**Scenario:** A software development team is building a reporting dashboard for a retail executive.

* **The Stakeholder (CEO):** Requests a "real-time view of inventory health." By "real-time," the CEO means data updated every morning before the 8:00 AM briefing.
* **The Technical Team:** Interprets "real-time" as a sub-second WebSocket integration that streams every barcode scan directly to the UI.
* **The Communication Gap:** The team spends three weeks and $50k extra building a complex data streaming architecture that the CEO didn't actually require.
* **Outcome:** The project goes over budget. The CEO is overwhelmed by a "jittery" dashboard that moves too fast for them to read during their morning coffee.

## Example 2: The "Implicit Assumption" Gap
**Scenario:** An architectural firm is designing a new corporate headquarters for a client.

* **The Stakeholder (Facilities Manager):** Assumes that "sustainability" automatically includes a high-end greywater recycling system because the architect mentioned a "green certification."
* **The Architect:** Assumes that "sustainability" refers only to LED lighting and high-efficiency HVAC units to stay within the client's strict construction budget.
* **The Communication Gap:** Neither party explicitly defined the scope of "Sustainability" in the initial Requirement Traceability Matrix (RTM).
* **Outcome:** During the late construction phase, the Facilities Manager discovers there is no water recycling system. Retrofitting the building costs triple the original estimate, leading to legal disputes over contract ambiguity.
