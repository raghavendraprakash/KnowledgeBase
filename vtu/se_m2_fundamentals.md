# Fundamentals of Requirements Engineering

Requirements engineering (RE) is a crucial software engineering action that bridges the gap between the initial customer need and the final software design and construction [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf]. It adapts to the specific needs of the project, product, and the team performing the work [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].

## The Seven Tasks of Requirements Engineering

Requirements engineering encompasses seven distinct tasks [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf]:

1.  **Inception:** The project begins when a business need or market opportunity is identified [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
    * *Example:* A retail company recognizes the need for a mobile application to increase online sales and identifies its scope and feasibility [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
2.  **Elicitation:** This task involves gathering requirements from stakeholders [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
    * *Example:* Using context-free questions to understand why a customer wants a new system, who will use it, and what the economic benefit will be [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
3.  **Elaboration:** Information from inception and elicitation is expanded and refined to build a detailed requirements model [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
    * *Example:* Developing user scenarios and identifying analysis classes to understand how end-users will interact with the system [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
4.  **Negotiation:** Stakeholders often have conflicting requirements due to limited resources; they must agree on a set of priorities [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
    * *Example:* Reconciling conflicting demands between the marketing team and the engineering team by ranking requirements and assessing cost/risk to reach a "win-win" result [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
5.  **Specification:** The requirements are documented in a formal or informal way, such as a written document, graphical models, or prototypes [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
    * *Example:* Creating a "Software Requirement Specification" (SRS) document that combines natural language descriptions with UML diagrams [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
6.  **Validation:** The requirements model is reviewed to ensure it is consistent, complete, and unambiguous [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
    * *Example:* Reviewing the requirements against objectives to ensure each requirement is testable and achievable [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
7.  **Management:** This involves managing changes to requirements as the software evolves [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].

## Software Engineering Definitions

* **Requirements Engineering:** A broad spectrum of tasks and techniques that leads to an understanding of requirements [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
* **Stakeholder:** Anyone who benefits in a direct or indirect way from the system being developed [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
* **Analysis Model:** A snapshot of requirements at any given time, providing a description of the required informational, functional, and behavioral domains [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
* **Use Case:** A story depicting software interaction from the end-user's point of view, focusing on how they interact with the system under specific circumstances [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
* **Data Object:** A representation of composite information that the software must understand, manipulate, and transform [cite: uploaded:02_Module-2.pdf].
