# Role Play: Collaborative Requirements Gathering

**Scenario:** A development team is meeting with stakeholders to define requirements for a new "Smart Home Energy Management System."

**Characters:**
* **Facilitator:** Sarah (Neutral, keeps the meeting on track).
* **Stakeholder 1 (Marketing):** David (Wants as many features as possible).
* **Stakeholder 2 (Engineering):** Elena (Concerned about technical feasibility and deadlines).
* **Stakeholder 3 (End User Rep):** Mike (Focuses on ease of use).

---

**Script:**

**Sarah (Facilitator):** Welcome, everyone. We are here to define the core requirements for the Smart Home Energy system. Our goal is to list features, prioritize them, and keep our constraints in mind. David, let's start with your vision.

**David (Marketing):** I want it to control everything—lights, heating, appliances—and it needs a mobile app with full predictive analytics.

**Elena (Engineering):** David, predictive analytics for every appliance is a massive undertaking for the initial version. We have a three-month development window.

**Mike (User Rep):** From a user perspective, simple controls for lights and heating are more important than complex analytics. Let's make sure the basic interface isn't cluttered.

**Sarah (Facilitator):** Okay, we have a potential conflict. David, if we prioritize the mobile app's core functions first, can we push the advanced predictive analytics to a second phase?

**David (Marketing):** If we can guarantee the basic app is perfect and intuitive, I suppose that’s acceptable.

**Elena (Engineering):** If we cut the predictive analytics for phase one, I can definitely deliver a robust, stable app within the three-month deadline.

**Sarah (Facilitator):** Excellent. Mike, how do you feel about this compromise?

**Mike (User Rep):** As long as the interface remains clean, that sounds like a great starting point for our users.

**Sarah (Facilitator):** Perfect. Let's capture these agreements in our mini-specs and proceed with the high-level design.

---

### Key Takeaways for Collaborative Gathering
* **The Facilitator’s Role:** Crucial for managing conflicts and ensuring all voices are heard without letting one stakeholder dominate.
* **Negotiation in Action:** Balancing the "what" (Marketing) with the "how/when" (Engineering) based on user needs.
* **Mini-specs:** Used as a quick way to document agreements made during the meeting to ensure everyone stays aligned.
