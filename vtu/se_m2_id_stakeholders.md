# Identifying Stakeholders: A Practical Guide

Stakeholder identification is the process of finding any individual, group, or organization that can affect or be affected by a project. A common mistake is only looking at the "obvious" users.

## The "Coffee Shop Expansion" Example

To understand how to identify stakeholders, let's look at a local coffee shop owner, "Bean & Leaf," who wants to open a second location with a drive-thru.

### Step 1: Brainstorming Categories
Instead of listing names, we start with categories of influence:
* **Internal:** People working inside the business.
* **External:** People outside the business.
* **Direct:** People who use the product/service every day.
* **Indirect:** People affected by the business's presence.

### Step 2: Mapping the Coffee Shop Stakeholders

| Stakeholder Group | Why they are a stakeholder (Impact/Interest) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Store Manager & Baristas** | Their daily workflow and jobs will change with the new equipment and drive-thru. |
| **Customers** | They gain a new location but might experience longer wait times if the drive-thru is prioritized. |
| **Local Neighbors** | They might be unhappy about increased traffic or noise from the drive-thru speakers. |
| **Suppliers** | They need to provide double the amount of coffee beans and milk. |
| **City Council/Zoning Board** | They must approve the permits for building and traffic flow. |
| **Competitors** | Nearby cafes will likely see a drop in their own morning traffic. |

### Step 3: Use the "Power-Interest" Grid
Once identified, we plot them to see how to handle them:
* **High Power / High Interest (Manage Closely):** The City Council (can stop the project) and the Store Manager (runs the operation).
* **Low Power / High Interest (Keep Informed):** Neighbors and regular customers.

## The Identification Checklist
When identifying stakeholders for any project (like your automated parking system), ask these four questions:
1.  **Who will use the system?** (Direct Users)
2.  **Who provides the resources for the system?** (Sponsors/Suppliers)
3.  **Who regulates the environment the system lives in?** (Government/Legal)
4.  **Who is nearby and might be bothered by it?** (Negative Stakeholders)
